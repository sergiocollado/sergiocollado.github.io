My experience in the Linux Kernel Mentorship program

In 2024 I participated in the Linux Kernel Mentorship Program, as a way to improve my kwnoledge about the Linux Kernel, my profile is here

Some references that I used from previous people that participated on the LKMP:

I contributed in two areas: backporting and translations.

Backporting

For backporting, I took advantage that in syskaller, the backport list was introduced. I asked the people form syskaller for pointers to attemp their backports, and they answered me here.

As references to do the backports I used:

  1. https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/next/process/backporting.html#submitting-backports-to-stable
  2. https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/next/process/backporting.html

The backports I submitted and were accepted in the linux stable 6.1.y were:

Translations

For the transalations I started to translate the documentation related to the scheduler to spanish. The main reason is to gain knowledge into the kernel scheduler. I assumed that doing a contribution here was quite challenging while doing a translation would help me to build knwoledge in the system. The translations that I submitted and were accepted are:
