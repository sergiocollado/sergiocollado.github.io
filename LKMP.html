My experience in the Linux Kernel Mentorship Program 2024

In 2024 I participated in the Linux Kernel Mentorship Program, as a way to improve my knowledge about the Linux Kernel, my profile can be found here

Some references that I used from people that had previously participated on the LKMP or have published interesting info for the program:

  • Javier Carrasco Cruz
  • Ricardo B. Marliere
  • Andrew Kanner

Other useful references were:

  • Linux Kernel Mentorship: Linux Kernel debugging Tricks of the Trade
  • Kernel Recipes 2019 - Hunting and fixing bugs all over the Linux kernel
  • submitting your first patch to the linux kernel and responding to feedback
  • My patch review workflow

I contributed in two areas: backporting and translations.

Backporting

For backporting, I took advantage that in syzkaller, the backport list was introduced. I asked the people from syzkaller for pointers to attemp their backports, and they answered me here.

As references to do the backports I used:

  1. https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/next/process/backporting.html#submitting-backports-to-stable
  2. https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/next/process/backporting.html
  3. https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/latest/process/stable-kernel-rules.html

The backports I submitted and were accepted in the linux stable 6.1.y were:

  • https://lore.kernel.org/stable/2024072519-among-surrogate-8c18@gregkh/T/#t
  • https://lore.kernel.org/stable/20240725133559.151607-1-sergio.collado@gmail.com/T/#u
  • https://lore.kernel.org/stable/20240725143111.222429-1-sergio.collado@gmail.com/T/#u

Translations

For the transalations I started to translate the documentation related to the scheduler to spanish. The main reason is to gain knowledge into the kernel scheduler. I assumed that doing a contribution here was quite challenging while doing a translation would help me to build knowledge in the system. The translations that I submitted and were accepted are:

  • https://lore.kernel.org/linux-doc/20240707195047.14359-1-sergio.collado@gmail.com/T/#u
  • https://lore.kernel.org/linux-doc/20240810111513.20049-1-sergio.collado@gmail.com/T/#u
  • https://lore.kernel.org/linux-doc/874j5niz5n.fsf@trenco.lwn.net/T/#t

Other patches

I also send with this kunit test: https://lore.kernel.org/rust-for-linux/20241117195923.222145-1-sergio.collado@gmail.com/T/#u but it wasn't accepted yet.

With regard to the translations, I would like to complete the translation to spanish of all the documents related to the scheduler.

Main takeaways

I would mention a deeper understanding of the kernel development cycle, and realizing it is not so difficult as it seemed previously. So I gained confidence for keep contributing further.
